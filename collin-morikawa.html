Career

During his college years, Collin began his amateur career. In 2013, he won the Western Junior. Following that, he won the Trans-Mississippi Amateur in 2015. He won the Silicon Valley Amateur and the Sunnehanna Amateur in 2016. In the same year, he was defeated by Ollie Schniederjans and J. J. Spaun in the Air Capital Classic. After a year, he won the ASU Thunderbird Invitational and the Northeast Amateur.

He also won the Arnold Palmer Cup twice (in 2017 and 2018), the Walker Cup once, and the Eisenhower Trophy once (2018). In addition, for three weeks in 2018, he was the top-ranked golfer in the World Amateur Golf Ranking. In 2018, he won the Wyoming Desert Intercollegiate, the Querencia Cabo Collegiate, and the Annual Western Intercollegiate. He went on to win 2019 The Farms Invitational as well as the Pac-12 Championship. In addition, he won the Barracuda Championship with a score of 47 points (13-7-13-14), finishing second to Troy Merritt of the United States.

Collin Morikawa made his professional debut at the 2019 RBC Canadian Open, finishing 14th. He finished second in the 3M Open and fourth in the John Deere Classic the same year. He later signed a PGA Tour contract for the 2019-20 season. He won his first PGA Tour event, the Barracuda Championship, by three strokes over Troy Merritt two weeks later.

He was tied for first place after 72 holes in the 2020 Charles Schwab Challenge in June 2020. The PGA Tour resumed play after a three-month hiatus due to the COVID-19 Pandemic. He was defeated by Daniel Berger after missing a very short par putt on the first playoff hole. He won the 2020 PGA Championship on August 9, 2020, his first major tournament victory in only his second major championship appearance. His final-round score of 64 tied Steve Elkington’s performance in the 1995 PGA Championship for the lowest final-round score by a PGA Champion.

On February 28, 2021, he won the WGC-Workday Championship at The Concession Golf Club in Bradenton, Florida. Furthermore, he won by three shots over Billy Horschel, Viktor Hovland, and Brooks Koepka. On July 18, he won the 2021 Open Championship at Royal St George’s Golf Club in Kent, England. He also defeated Jordan Spieth by two strokes. Collin became the first player to win two major championships in as few as eight starts since Bobby Jones in 1926. In addition, he became the first player in history to win two majors in his debut.

In August, he tied for third place in the Olympic Games. He was also eliminated in a seven-man playoff for the bronze medal. He won the DP World Tour Championship in Dubai in November, capping off the European Tour season. In addition, he became the first American to win the Race to Dubai. In February 2022, he shot a final-round 65 to tie for second place at the Genesis Invitational, two shots behind Joaqun Niemann.
